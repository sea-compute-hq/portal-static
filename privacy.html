Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 15, 2026

1. Data Controller

SEA-COMPUTE ("we", "us", "our") is the data controller for personal data collected through this platform. We operate as a cross-border cloud computing service reseller.

2. Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of personal data:

  • Account data: email address, username, hashed password
  • Payment data: transaction references (we do not store full payment credentials)
  • Usage data: IP address, browser type, access times, pages viewed
  • Communication data: support tickets, email correspondence

3. Legal Basis for Processing (GDPR Art. 6)

We process personal data based on:

  • Contract performance (Art. 6(1)(b)): to provide cloud computing services
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)): fraud prevention, service improvement
  • Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c)): regulatory compliance, tax records
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): marketing communications (withdrawable at any time)

4. Data Retention

Account data is retained for the duration of your account plus 2 years for legal and tax compliance. Usage logs are retained for 90 days. Payment records are retained for 7 years per applicable tax regulations.

5. Your Rights (GDPR)

Under the General Data Protection Regulation, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Rectify inaccurate data
  • Request erasure of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restrict or object to processing
  • Data portability
  • Withdraw consent at any time
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@sea-compute.com.

6. International Data Transfers

As a cross-border service, your data may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your region of residence. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) where required.

7. Sanctioned Regions

SEA-COMPUTE does not provide services to individuals or entities located in sanctioned jurisdictions, including: Russia, Iran, DPRK (North Korea), Cuba, and Syria. We do not knowingly collect or process personal data from individuals in these regions. If we discover such data, it will be promptly deleted.

8. Cookies

We use essential cookies for authentication and session management. Analytics cookies are used to improve our service. You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings.

9. Third-Party Processors

We engage the following third-party service providers: cloud infrastructure providers (for compute resource delivery), payment processors (for transaction handling), and analytics providers. All processors are bound by data processing agreements compliant with GDPR Article 28.

10. Contact & DPO

For privacy-related inquiries or to contact our Data Protection Officer: privacy@sea-compute.com
