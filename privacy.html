Privacy Policy

Effective: 2026-07-15 · Last updated: 2026-07-15

SEA-COMPUTE ("we", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what data we collect, why we collect it, how we use it, and what rights you have. This policy is designed to be compatible with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and other applicable data protection laws.

§1. Data Controller

SEA-COMPUTE is the data controller for personal data collected through this platform. Contact: sea.compute.hp+compliance@gmail.com

§2. Data We Collect

Category Examples When Collected
Account Email, username, password hash Registration
Identity (KYC) Name, date of birth, ID document KYC-Lite verification
Financial Payment method, transaction history Top-up, withdrawal
Technical IP address, browser, device info Every session
Usage Instance types, duration, spend During service use

§3. KYC-Lite Verification

We use a graduated verification model to balance regulatory compliance with user convenience:

Stage 1 — Basic (Required for all accounts)

Email verification + valid payment method. No identity documents required.

Stage 2 — Enhanced (Triggered by usage patterns)

Required when: cumulative spend exceeds USD $500, or withdrawal requests exceed USD $200/month. Requires government-issued photo ID.

Stage 3 — Full (For high-volume or business accounts)

Required when: monthly spend exceeds USD $5,000, or operating as a business entity. Requires proof of address + source of funds declaration.

§4. Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

We are required to screen users against sanctions lists (OFAC, EU, UN) and monitor for suspicious transaction patterns. This is a legal obligation and not a discretionary practice.

Screening occurs at registration and periodically thereafter. If a match is found, the account may be restricted pending manual review.

§5. Excluded Markets

Due to international sanctions and regulatory restrictions, we do not provide services to users located in or resident of:

  • Russia
  • Iran
  • Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK / North Korea)
  • Cuba
  • Syria
  • Other OFAC-sanctioned jurisdictions

Accounts found to be operated from these jurisdictions will be suspended and funds returned via the original payment method where possible.

§6. Data Retention

Data Type Retention Period Basis

Financial records 10 years Tax / AML law
Technical logs (IP, sessions) 180 days Legitimate interest
Communication records 3 years Dispute resolution

§7. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights:

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate data
  • Erasure: Delete your data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interest
  • Restriction: Limit processing in certain circumstances

To exercise these rights, email sea.compute.hp+compliance@gmail.com. We respond within 30 days.

§8. Data Sharing

We share data with:

  • Payment processors: To process top-ups and withdrawals
  • Upstream providers: Only the minimum necessary to provision instances (e.g., SSH keys, region selection)
  • Regulators: When legally compelled by court order or regulatory directive
  • Sanctions screening services: For AML compliance

We do not sell your data to third parties. We do not use your data for advertising.

§9. International Transfers

Our infrastructure and partners operate across multiple jurisdictions. When transferring data outside the EEA, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or adequacy decisions. You may request a copy of the safeguards in place.

§10. Security & Breach Notification

We implement industry-standard security measures including encryption at rest and in transit, access controls, and regular security audits.

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in harm to you, we will notify affected users within 72 hours of discovery, via email to the address on file.

§11. Changes to This Policy

We will notify users of material changes via email at least 14 days before they take effect. Continued use of the Service after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
